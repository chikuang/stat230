5.1 Combination

Definition 5.1 (Combination) Given \(n\) distinct objects, a combination of size \(k\) is an unordered subset of \(k\) of the individuals. The number of combinations of size \(k\) taken from \(n\) objects is denoted \({n \choose k}\) or \({}_n C_k\) and can be computed as \[ {n \choose k}=\frac{n^{(k)}}{k!}=\frac{n!}{(n-k)!\ k!}. \]

  • The number \({n \choose k}\) is called Binomial coefficient.
  • Please do find out how to access this on your calculator!

Set v.s. Tuples

  • If you do not care about the order, the sample space \(S\) consists of sets \(\{1,2,3\}\), \(\{1,2,4\}\),and note that we are counting \(\{1,2,3\}\) and \(\{2,1,3\}\) as the same outcome.
  • If you do consider the order, the sample space \(S\) consists of tuples of the form \((1,2,3), (2,1,3), (1,3,2), (1,2,4),\dots\) and note that \((1,2,3)\) and \((2,1,3)\) are counted as two different outcomes.
